Digital Literacy

Refers to a particular set of competencies that allow individuals to function and participate fully in a digital world. This includes understanding how to use various digital devices and software applications, as well as being able to communicate, collaborate, and engage with digital content and communities in a responsible and ethical manner.

The List Components of Digital Literacy Skills

Critical Thinking-The ability to navigate, understand, and utilize this digital landscape has become crucial in an age where the digital world is as rich and complex as the physical. This is where digital literacy steps in, acting as a compass to guide us through the information, tools, and interactions available online.

  • Analyze and evaluate information and arguments

  • See patterns and connections,

  • Identify and build meaningful information

  • "Find out whether this is true or just fiction; make sure it's legitimate and not fake news."

Online Safety Skills

Being safe online means that you have the knowledge to identify the potential risks and are conscious of your personal security while browsing, sharing or surfing the internet. By teaching internet safety, you are not only helping your students become more vigilant about their own safety, but also helping them become better users of the internet.

  • Cyber-bullying

  • Sexting

  • Age-appropriate content

  • Photo sharing and permission

Finding Information

The internet has provided us with a wealth of information at our fingertips. The problem with this is, we almost have too much information to choose from. It is sometimes difficult to sort between real information, misinformation, opinions, and bias. Most of us rely on search engines to do the sorting for us but this also isn’t enough. Students need to know how to search accurately and then to evaluate the information they find, our critical thinking guide will help you develop these skills.When researching online independently students may rely on a quick Google or Wikipedia search, followed by copy and paste!

  • From starting your search to verifying the information you find, here are 4 tips for better online research.

  • Think before you begin.

  • Where to start your search?

  • When looking at search results, dig deep – don’t stop at the first page!

  • Use special search functions to make the search engines work for you

Digital Literacy Quiz

1. What is the best way to verify the credibility of an online source?

2. Which of the following is an example of cyberbullying?

3. What should you do before sharing a photo online?

4. What is a good first step when starting online research?

5. Which of the following tools can enhance collaboration in digital projects?

6. What is the benefit of using digital platforms for teamwork?

7. How can you ensure the technical proficiency of using a new software?

8. What is a sign of reliable online information?

9. Why is it important to use age-appropriate content online?

10. What is a good practice when creating digital content?

11. How can you determine if a post on social media is true or just fiction?

12. How can you determine if what you copy and paste from Google can be used in your research or tasks?
